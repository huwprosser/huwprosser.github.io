Easily Accessible

The open web is honestly a miracle. The fact that I can go into my grandmother's home, fire up a big old beige PC and access a website made yesterday is mind-blowing. What's more mind-blowing is that I can open up that very same website on a brand new iPhone 14 Pro max and send messages and content to the old beige paperweight despite them being 20 years apart in age. Web allows anyone to talk to a Carter character on any device, or in any browser - including an in-app browser like TikTok's, a Raspberry Pi, a custom Electron desktop app etc.

Restrictions

Apps are amazing, but they're extremely slow to maintain, restricted by large corporations and by individual device capabilities. This is especially hard when trying to use a microphone, camera etc. And then, if you want to take that app and run it on a Raspberry Pi or a PC you almost always end up re-writing something from scratch. Web apps can roll out changes much faster and without unnecessary scrutiny. Building a plugin mechanism within an iOS app, for example, would be much too restrictive for what we have planned for Carter. The open web also allows us to simulate any character, conversation, or relationship we want.

Future Proof

This is the big one. The browser in recent years has gained a lot of horsepower due to incredible new web standards. We're seeing game engines, ML on the edge and more due to this. WebGPU, for example, is becoming supported across many devices. It allows websites to better access the hardware they're running on, specifically the GPU. Great for gaming in the browserâ€¦but also great for building truly revolutionary AI-powered experiences. WebXR gives websites the ability to run augmented and virtual reality content - on a phone it's a little awkwardâ€¦but on a Quest 3 or an Apple Vision Pro this could really change how we think about the web and the virtual people that live there.

Intriguing Potential Future Side-quests

Did you know that VSCode, Discord, Spotify, and Slack are all actually just downloadable browsers that run one web app? Electron and other technologies have started pulling web tech off the web and onto the local OS. Who knows what could mean down the line with a desktop Carter Chat and enhanced hardware connectivity. The developer ecosystem around web is weird, wacky, and powerful, it will lead to great things for Carter.

We're building a place where creators can bring characters to life, customize and enhance them with plugins and share them with non-developers on any device. On top of that, every character will have first-class text and voice capabilities that run on any device - deeper emotions, much better memory and a few other things we're not quite ready to announce yet.
