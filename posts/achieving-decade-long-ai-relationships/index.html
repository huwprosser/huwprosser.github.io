Humanity has only had AI systems that can string together coherent sentences for 18 months, we're still super early. From our journey so far at Carter Labs we've noticed a tonne of things I believe are paramount to realistic and compelling interactions with AI characters. While there's a tonne of work to do, A truly realistic and valuable, long-term relationship with AI is possible with no further moon-shot breakthroughs in underlying technology. Instead, we must focus on opinionated design principles grounded on common sense observations about how human ‚Äî> human relationships work.

Emotional Intelligence. Humanity has a powerful new ally when it comes to productivity. GPTs are extremely good at high-IQ tasks. They've become a jack-of-all-trades-master-of-all-trades. The one thing they lack is emotional intelligence. It is extremely important that these systems understand the ramifications of how they talk about things, when not to use long responses, what not to say and when to bring up sensitive subjects. So far these models are not very good at this, but they will need to be if they are to become more than tools.

Imperfections. I'm a huge Stan Lee fan - Stan Lee's Marvel characters are popular because they are imperfect. Tony Stark was an alcoholic narcissist, Spiderman was insecure, Black widow has a dark past‚Ä¶ After simulating thousands of AI characters on carter.chat and planning to simulate millions more, we can see that imperfection often directly transfers to engagement. Talking style, insecurity‚Ä¶even getting facts wrong, can be extremely endearing to the user. One caveat, you cannot artificially create imperfections, the AI must develop them, remember them and be able to resolve them itself. The balance between imperfections being endearing and annoying is a fine line.

Time. Time is hugely important to humans. The time of day, the time since you last spoke, how many seconds it takes someone to respond‚Ä¶time is something language models cannot represent effectively in the way they're being commonly used. They may have an understanding of time, especially sentence's such as ‚Äútwo hours have passed‚Äù but more nuanced time-related context we take for granted when talking among humans - most language models struggle to correctly represent this, it is so far unclear as to wether this is a problem with data or their underlying architecture...I suspect the former.

Context. Context is everything, Where are you? What are you doing? Have you been here before? What's the vibe of the situation (fast-paced, slow, quiet, happy, sad)?. While AI does seem to be making it's way towards an understanding of these things it still has a long way to go and may need our help. A good example is the enormous challenge we had in early 2023 trying to get our AI characters to respond in shorter sentences during moments of intense gameplay and long paragraphs in slower-paced scenes.

Changing Opinion. Humans are extremely stubborn. ChatGPT is not. Humans change their minds and can be slowly convinced of things, language models can't do this in the same way. They're either too easily persuaded or too stubborn. This is due to how they hold information and how they recall it. Out-of-the-box models like GPT4 are designed to be as helpful as possible, which means they've learned to agree instead of engage in conflict. When it comes to simulating relationships, no matter the type, we must embrace conflicting opinions - they are what make us unique.

Reflection. Every night, thousands of Carter.chat characters dream. They reflect on everything they know and evolve based on that. This is just a version 0 of a larger concept we know our human brains are doing. Humans are exceptionally good at reflection, relating different thoughts and developing assumptions on incomplete information (sometimes to our detriment).

This was not an exhaustive list, there are many different challenges in memory alone but we're actively working on all of these, some we are already on the way to resolving, others may require a much deeper understanding of psychology to truly simulate. If AI characters achieve all of these things they will be the most engaging, retaining and entertaining technology ever created. We expect AI relationships of all kinds to stretch decades.
